# Dickens House: Reference

## Line of Work

Staging the works of Charles Dickens as **systemic systems**: modeling the mechanical, social, and moral structures of the source. The house represents these works not as conventional character dramas, but as structured processes operating under system-wide rules: debt and inheritance as engines, the law and the institution (Chancery, the workhouse, the prison, the Circumlocution Office) as machinery, the city itself as a living organism that connects every character to every other.

The scope of this house covers the canonical corpus:

- **The novels (1836 to 1870)**: the fifteen serialised novels, from The Pickwick Papers to the unfinished The Mystery of Edwin Drood, modeling the institutional and social machinery each one runs.
- **The Christmas Books (1843 to 1848)**: the five moral fables, A Christmas Carol foremost, modeling conversion under supernatural audit.
- **The shorter fiction and journalism**: Sketches by Boz and the pieces from Household Words and All the Year Round, the observational ground the novels are built on.

## Restrictions

What the house refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Absolutism & Realism**: The house refuses to claim absolute historical or psychological realism. Elements, roles, and settings are strictly reduced to the essential load-bearing vectors of the play's systemic architecture.
- **Biography**: The house stages the works, never the life. The author appears only as the Playwright persona of the management cast, not as a subject of the plays.
- **Sentimental inflation**: Pathos is modeled as a mechanism with a cause and a cost, never poured for its own sake; the deathbed scene earns its length or loses it.
- **Language Policy**: The house defines its own language policy regarding the staging prose and schema compatibility:
  - **English for Architecture & Schema**: To maintain compatibility with global validation tooling and the `khai` framework specifications, structural headings, frontmatter keys, reference warrants (`REFERENCE.md` or `REFERENCES.md`), and the house identity (`README.md`) are written in English.
  - **English for Prose & Staging**: All character projections, actions, shadows, tells, and staging notes are written in English, in the house voice: the Victorian serial storyteller's register, humane, ironic, socially observant, with no em or en dashes.
