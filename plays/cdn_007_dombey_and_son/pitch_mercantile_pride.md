# Pitch: Mercantile Pride

## Taxonomy

The tuning of tone for the whole play: a production directive.

## Owner

- Owner: [Dombey and Son](play_cdn_007_dombey_and_son.md)

## Tenor

A Victorian industrial register, contrasting the cold, metallic, proud offices of Dombey and Son and the screaming roar of the steam railway with the warm, wooden comfort of the Midshipman shop.

## Undertow

The relentless, rhythmic chugging of the steam train engine and the scratching of corporate ledger pens, echoing through the empty, dusty mansion rooms.

## Nerve

The reader's grief over the decline of fragile young Paul, and the dread of Carker's treacherous teeth closing on the proud house.

## Echo

A persistent assurance that cold pride and commercial ambition lead to absolute isolation, which only simple, domestic love can rescue.
