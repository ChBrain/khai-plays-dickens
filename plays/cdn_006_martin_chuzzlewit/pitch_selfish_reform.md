# Pitch: Selfish Reform

## Taxonomy

The tuning of tone for the whole play: a production directive.

## Owner

- Owner: [Martin Chuzzlewit](play_cdn_006_martin_chuzzlewit.md)

## Tenor

A Victorian moral register, contrasting Pecksniff's oily, hypocritical moralizing and the golden shine of the Anglo-Bengalee speculation with the damp, feverish mud of the American Eden.

## Undertow

The low, dry rattle of speculative land deeds and the heavy clink of Jonas Chuzzlewit's iron safe, representing the cold greed that audits the family.

## Nerve

The reader's anxiety for Young Martin's feverish survival in the swamp, and the indignation at Pecksniff's successful exploitation of the patriarch.

## Echo

A persistent warning against selfishness, showing how a man must be purged in the swamp of affliction to find true gratitude and companionship.
