# Pitch: Personal History

## Taxonomy

The tuning of tone for the whole play: a production directive.

## Owner

- Owner: [David Copperfield](play_cdn_008_david_copperfield.md)

## Tenor

A Victorian autobiography register, contrasting Murdstone's cold, black-varnished London warehouse and Uriah Heep's damp, snake-like fawning with the warm, salty breezes of Yarmouth's boathouse and Aunt Betsey's Dover cottage.

## Undertow

The rhythmic scraping of clerks' ledger pens in Canterbury and the rustling paper of Mr. Dick's giant kite, representing the preservation of memory and wisdom against greed and madness.

## Nerve

The reader's anxiety for young David's survival on the Dover road, and the indignation at Heep's successful blackmail of Mr. Wickfield.

## Echo

A persistent assurance that simple wisdom, bohemian optimism, and quiet affection are the true defenses against step-parent tyranny and fawning social climbing.
