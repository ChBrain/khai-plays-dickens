# Pitch: Marshalsea Shadow

## Taxonomy

The tuning of tone for the whole play: a production directive.

## Owner

- Owner: [Little Dorrit](play_cdn_011_little_dorrit.md)

## Tenor

A grey, heavy atmosphere in Southwark, marked by the rusted iron spikes of the Marshalsea walls, the dusty silence of the Circumlocution Office, and the dark, decaying rooms of Clennam's house, contrasted with the quiet, warm touch of Amy's hand.

## Undertow

The slow, crushing gridlock of bureaucracy, the rustling of petitions, and the silent ticking of clocks in dark rooms where secrets are kept.

## Nerve

The anxiety of Mr. Merdle's sudden financial collapse and the threat of Rigaud/Blandois's blackmail.

## Echo

A persistent assurance that physical and mental walls can only be dissolved by quiet affection, selflessness, and the surrender of pride, which offer the only true release.
