# Pitch: Fact and Fancy

## Taxonomy

The tuning of tone for the whole play: a production directive.

## Owner

- Owner: [Hard Times](play_cdn_010_hard_times.md)

## Tenor

An industrial landscape of black soot and red brick in Coketown, smelling of coal-smoke and warm oil, contrasted with the golden, sawdust-scented ring and joyful music of Sleary's Circus.

## Undertow

The monotonous, rhythmic beating of power-loom steam engines, turning the day into a series of identical facts, and the scratch of slate pencils in the schoolroom.

## Nerve

The emotional starvation of Louisa Gradgrind and the desperation of Tom's bank robbery framing.

## Echo

A persistent reminder that human affection, imagination, and play (Fancy) are as necessary for life as the Facts of political economy, and that the circus ring offers a truer sanctuary than the stone schoolroom.
