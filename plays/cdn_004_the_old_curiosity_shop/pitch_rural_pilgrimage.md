## Tenor

A Victorian moral register, contrasting the grotesque, aggressive antics of the London dwarf with the quiet, pastoral peace of the English countryside and ancient churchyards.

## Undertow

The roaring noise of industrial furnaces and Squeers-like schoolhouses, showing how the machinery of the Black Country audits the child's flight.

## Nerve

The reader's anxiety for Nell's failing health, set against the relentless, shadow-like pursuit of Daniel Quilp.

## Echo

A persistent, elegiac reminder that pure innocence and quiet sanctuary, though fragile in life, are preserved forever in death.
