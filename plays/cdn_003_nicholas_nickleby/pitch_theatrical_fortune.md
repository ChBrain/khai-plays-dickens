# Pitch: Theatrical Fortune

## Taxonomy

The tuning of tone for the whole play: a production directive.

## Owner

- Owner: [Nicholas Nickleby](play_cdn_003_nicholas_nickleby.md)

## Tenor

A Victorian theatrical register, full of declamation, fencing matches, and the broad comedy of Portsmouth players, contrasted with the grim, cold realities of Yorkshire Squeers.

## Undertow

The slow, quiet whisper of the counting-house usurer and Squeers' dry cough, waiting to squeeze the brother and sister of the house.

## Nerve

The reader's indignation at the starvation of Yorkshire orphans, and the dread of Ralph's calculations that threaten the innocent.

## Echo

A persistent belief that energetic youth, honest labor, and benevolent companions can defeat the coldest schemes of avarice.
