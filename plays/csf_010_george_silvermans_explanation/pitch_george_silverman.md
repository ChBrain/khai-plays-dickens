# Pitch: George Silverman

## Taxonomy

The tuning of tone for the whole play: a production directive.

## Owner

- Owner: [George Silverman's Explanation](play_csf_010_george_silvermans_explanation.md)

## Tenor

A quiet, melancholy, and psychologically complex first-person monologue: the damp, cold Preston cellars, the loud, hypocritical boasts of Brother Hawkyard, the quiet academic chambers of Cambridge, and the silent, self-sacrificing heart.

## Undertow

The slow, monotonous dripping of cellar water, the crackle of a sparse study fire, the scratching of a confession pen, the dry rustle of financial ledgers, and the quiet sighs of unexpressed love.

## Nerve

The painful, quiet martyrdom of a selfless man whose every act of charity is misunderstood as hypocrisy and self-interest.

## Echo

A persistent warning that the loudest, most public displays of religious charity are often covers for greed, while true self-sacrifice must accept public misunderstanding in silence.
