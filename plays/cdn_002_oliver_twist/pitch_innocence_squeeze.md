# Pitch: Innocence Squeeze

## Taxonomy

The tuning of tone for the whole play: a production directive.

## Owner

- Owner: [Oliver Twist](play_cdn_002_oliver_twist.md)

## Tenor

A Victorian moral register, contrasting the cold, administrative vocabulary of the parish workhouse with the dark, slang-filled shadows of Fagin's den.

## Undertow

The low, threatening growl of Sikes's dog and the heavy clink of Fagin's keys, representing the constant pressure of vice surrounding Oliver.

## Nerve

The reader's anxiety for Oliver's fragile innocence, constantly squeezed by workhouse boards, thief-catchers, and housebreakers.

## Echo

A persistent warning against social indifference, showing how a child's soul must be shielded from the machinery of poverty.
