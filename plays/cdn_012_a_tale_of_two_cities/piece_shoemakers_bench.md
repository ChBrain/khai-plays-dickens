# Piece: Shoemakers Bench

## Taxonomy

The symbols of psychological escape: a shoemaker's bench.

## Owner

- Project: [A Tale of Two Cities](play_cdn_012_a_tale_of_two_cities.md)

## Place

[Wine Shop](place_wine_shop.md)

## Load Bearing

Dr. Manette's shoemaking habit. Without this shoemaker's bench and tools representing his psychological escape from the trauma of the Bastille, his character development collapses.

## Apparent

A small wooden shoemaker's bench, with a hammer, awl, leather scraps, and unfinished shoes, worn by years of obsessive use.

## Yearbook

- In the Bastille cell, Dr. Manette began using this bench to stay sane.
- Today, he hammer leather above the Paris wine shop, recognizing no one.
- The bench is destroyed by his friends in London to prevent a relapse.
